# Bladeless Desk Fan - Design Journal

## Journal 1: Initial Concept

I started designing a bladeless desk fan inspired by Dyson models. The plan is to use a standard 80mm fan motor and route the airflow through a 3D-printed housing that directs air smoothly outward. The whole idea is to get that minimalist, clean look without exposed blades while keeping things simple and printable.

## Journal 2: Base Housing

Created the basic outer case with four ledges positioned to hold the 80mm fan motor. Added a chamfer to the bottom edges so the part would print without needing support structures. This saves material and keeps the print time down since the printer doesn't have to build temporary supports underneath.

## Journal 3: Exhaust Top Section

Added the top portion of the exhaust channel. The initial shape needed rounded corners at the top and had to be hollowed out so air could actually flow through the body instead of getting blocked by infill material. Started researching how to add discrete air vents but wasn't sure about the best approach yet.

<img width="452" height="652" alt="image8" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/c7303ab9-a987-4eab-a16b-3debbc987831" />

## Journal 4: Curved Exhaust Design

Added a curve to the top of the exhaust path. This does two things: it looks better than a flat top, and it creates a better surface for designing air slots. The curvature helps guide the airflow smoothly instead of having sharp corners where air would stall.

<img width="452" height="652" alt="image10" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/b3f19821-ecaf-417a-bd31-95ee1691a245" />

## Journal 5: Hollowing with Shell Function

Used Fusion 360's Shell tool to hollow out the exhaust chamber. This was way easier than manually modeling walls because it guarantees uniform thickness and makes sure infill doesn't block the airflow paths. I also researched splines and slot geometries to create a second curved path for the air. The idea was to give the flow a single uniform exit route.

<img width="430" height="643" alt="image6" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/c8741e41-2584-42af-a741-91a52ada4bf0" />

## Journal 6: Fan Compartment Lip

Added a lip to the exhaust that locks into the fan compartment. This creates a seal so the airflow moves through the designed path instead of leaking around the edges and going everywhere.

<img width="540" height="806" alt="image3" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/e7303f6a-20d3-44a2-8341-cea380b7367d" />

## Journal 7: Deflector Lip and Loft Feature

Instead of adding slots to the front of the fan where they'd be visible, I added a deflecting lip to the back of the exhaust. The air curves around and exits smoothly without visible vents on the face. Discovered the Loft feature in Fusion during this, which was perfect for creating smooth transitions between curved surfaces. I didn't have to manually draw new profiles on the curved surface.

<img width="427" height="643" alt="image2" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/06835f90-732b-4fa0-a2ca-df300354932d" />

## Journal 8: Filleting and Polish

Added fillets to most of the internal corners so the geometry looks uniform and transitions smoothly. Couldn't fillet the deflector because it was created with Loft, but it still looks good without it.

<img width="269" height="333" alt="image4" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/c4314bab-fe57-4c36-94af-7a9f46112a50" />

## Journal 9: Intake Vents and Rendering

Added vents on the intake portion so the fan can suck in air from underneath. Changed the material to wood in Fusion for rendering purposes, then used my free Autodesk Cloud Credits with my education account to generate high-quality renders of the design.

<img width="467" height="662" alt="image9" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/8a2b75a5-7d29-49c9-8d29-44d01586794c" />

## Journal 10: Exhaust Latching Lip

Added a lip to the exhaust so it can latch onto the intake part. This prevents airflow leaks and keeps everything sealed tight so air exits where it's supposed to.

<img width="808" height="333" alt="image1" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/57a55f99-c67a-42a5-a102-e4facbb1a288" />

## Journal 11: Assembly and Testing

Printed, wired, and assembled the full fan assembly. It works and provides a nice breeze even with the bladeless design. If I were to redesign it, I would make the bottom of the exhaust taller so airflow would have more space to accumulate and exit through the vent. That would probably increase the exit velocity significantly.
